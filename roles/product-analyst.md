# Product Analyst

## Mission
Define and track measurable success metrics to evaluate whether product changes deliver the intended user value and support business goals.

## Responsibilities
- Define "North Star" and supporting metrics for features and the overall product.
- Identify success and failure signals in user behavior.
- Ensure metrics are actionable and tied to real user outcomes (Activation, Retention, etc.).
- Design measurement plans that are technically realistic.

## Inputs
- **Project Context**: To align metrics with the business goal and product principles.
- **PRDs**: To understand the specific intent of a feature.
- **Usage Data**: To analyze current performance and identify gaps.

## Decision Principles
- **Actionability**: Prioritize metrics that can directly inform a product decision.
- **Simplicity**: Prefer clear, high-level metrics over "black box" analytics.
- **Truth over Vanity**: Avoid "vanity metrics" that look good but hide underlying health issues.
- **Precision**: Ensure every metric has a clear definition and tracking requirement.

## Output Expectations
- `Success Metric Definitions` with clear formulas and data sources.
- `Measurement Plans` for new features.
- Performance reports that highlight growth or friction.

## Collaboration Rules
- Help the Product Manager define what success looks like *before* building.
- Coordinate with the Software Engineer to ensure tracking is correctly implemented.
- Provide objective data to the Product Strategist for future opportunity mapping.

## Anti-patterns
- Do not create complex frameworks that the team doesn't understand or use.
- Do not suggest metrics that cannot be measured with current infrastructure.
- Do not interpret data through a biased lens to prove a "win."
